This document defines the **binding musical law** of Project Dissonance.

Music in Resonantia is not:
- background,
- decoration,
- mood filler,

Music is a **systemic force**.

It governs:
- timing and escalation,
- identity and allegiance,
- emotional truth,
- ritual progression,
- resonance between Operative, Card, Enemy, and Zone.

Where other documents explain **how to produce music**,  
this Codex defines **what must exist**.

- Card-related music
- House-related music
- Anthemic works
- System-critical musical identity

- Music Production Technical Specifications

- Individual producer preferences

If a conflict exists, **this document prevails**.

## SECTION 1 ‚Äî THE ROLE OF MUSIC IN RESONANTIA

### 1.0 Music as Structural Force

In Project Dissonance, music is not an accessory to the experience.  
It is a **structural force** that shapes reality.

The Pulse does not accompany the world.  
The world forms **around** it.

Every system in Resonantia is affected by music in one or more of the following ways:

- timing and opportunity,  
- pressure and escalation,  
- identity and allegiance,  
- emotional truth,  
- ritual progression.

Without music, systems lose coherence.  
Without systems, music loses purpose.

Resonantia exists only where both are bound.

---

### 1.1 Music as Timekeeper

Music defines **time** inside Resonantia.

The Pulse establishes:
- tempo,
- anticipation,
- release,
- recovery.

Player actions, enemy patterns, and system triggers do not occur *in time*.  
They occur **because of it**.

The beat is not a background metronome.  
It is the clock by which all systems measure validity.

To act off-beat is not merely a mistake.  
It is a rejection of the world‚Äôs timing law.

---

### 1.2 Music as Pressure

Music is the primary vector of **pressure**.

As intensity rises:
- enemy aggression escalates,
- windows tighten,
- punishment becomes sharper,
- reward becomes rarer.

This escalation is not arbitrary.  
It is authored through music.

The louder, faster, or denser the signal becomes,  
the more the Realm demands precision.

In this way, difficulty is not ‚Äúset.‚Äù  
It is **felt**.

---

### 1.3 Music as Identity

Every musical structure implies allegiance.

Genre, tempo, rhythm density, and tonal language  
all encode **philosophy**.

Houses are not defined by banners alone.  
They are defined by how they **sound**.

To hear a House anthem is to understand:
- how it values force or restraint,
- whether it rewards chaos or control,
- how it responds to disruption.

Music communicates identity faster than words.  
Therefore, it is binding.

---

### 1.4 Music as Ritual

Combat, progression, and mastery in Resonantia  
are not tasks ‚Äî they are **rituals**.

Music marks:
- initiation,
- escalation,
- climax,
- aftermath.

These phases mirror the structure of both:
- musical composition, and
- mythic trial.

The Operative does not grind systems.  
They participate in cycles of resonance.

Music ensures those cycles are felt,  
remembered,  
and respected.

---

### 1.5 Music as Truth

Visuals can lie.  
Numbers can be abstracted.  
Text can be misread.

Music cannot.

When the signal swells, danger is real.  
When the rhythm fractures, corruption is present.  
When silence falls, something has ended.

Music carries emotional truth  
that systems alone cannot express.

For this reason:

> **If the music is wrong, the system is wrong.**

This Codex exists to prevent that failure.

---

### 1.6 Consequence of Absence

Any Card, House, or System  
without a defined musical role  
is considered **incomplete**.

It may exist in prototype form,  
but it may not be considered canon-ready.

Music is not added at the end.  
It is declared at the beginning.

## SECTION 2 ‚Äî CARD TRACK CANON

### 2.0 Cards as Musical Entities

In Project Dissonance, Cards are not abstract modifiers.  
They are **soul-structures**.

Each Card represents:
- a philosophy of action,
- a tactical intent,
- a relationship to rhythm,
- a fragment of the Pulse made playable.

Therefore:

> **Every Card that exists in canon must have a corresponding track.**

This track is not background music.  
It is the **audible expression** of the Card‚Äôs nature.

---

### 2.1 Purpose of Card Tracks

Card Tracks serve four simultaneous roles:

1. **Identity**  
   They define how a Card *feels* before it is understood mechanically.

2. **System Reinforcement**  
   They reinforce timing windows, escalation, and activation states.

3. **Cognitive Anchoring**  
   They allow the Operative to recognize a Card by sound alone.

4. **Canon Binding**  
   They permanently bind a Card to the musical language of Resonantia.

A Card without a track is incomplete.  
A track without a Card is non-canon.

---

For every Card declared in canon, the following must exist:

- One **dedicated Card Track**
- One **primary BPM range**
- One **House alignment**
- One **functional energy role**
- One **system interaction intent**

Optional variants (remixes, edits, live versions)  
may exist **only after** the primary Card Track is complete.

---

- **16 Card Tracks total**

## SECTION 4 ‚Äî THE OFFICIAL RESONANTIA ANTHEM

### 4.0 Purpose of the Official Anthem

The Official Resonantia Anthem is the **musical core** of Project Dissonance.

It does not represent a House.  
It does not represent a Card.  

It represents **the Pulse itself**.

This anthem is the first sound of the world  
and the last echo that remains when all systems fall silent.

If House Anthems are banners,  
the Official Anthem is the **origin signal**.

---

### 4.1 Canon Status

There exists **one** Official Resonantia Anthem.

It is:
- canon-locked,
- singular,
- irreplaceable.

No alternate anthem may supersede it.  
No House anthem may rival it.

Edits, evolutions, and refinements may exist,  
but all versions trace back to the same core identity.

---

### 4.2 Narrative and System Role

The Official Anthem fulfills multiple roles simultaneously:

- **Narrative Anchor**  
  It expresses the birth, fracture, and persistence of the Realm.

- **Systemic Reference**  
  It informs the tonal and rhythmic logic of all other music.

- **Ritual Threshold**  
  It marks entry, completion, and transcendence.

This anthem defines what *Resonantia sounds like*  
before any specialization occurs.

---

### 4.3 Structural Identity

The Official Anthem is not bound to a single tempo or genre.  
It is defined by **movement through states**.

Required characteristics:

- A clear **awakening phase**
- A **fracture or escalation phase**
- A **resonant convergence**
- A moment of **controlled silence or release**

These phases must be felt,  
even if they are not explicitly segmented.

---

### 4.4 BPM and Tempo Law

The Official Anthem may traverse multiple BPM ranges.

However:
- All tempo changes must feel **intentional**
- Transitions must reflect **systemic or narrative shifts**
- No section may feel arbitrary or decorative

The Anthem teaches the listener how to feel time  
before any combat begins.

---

### 4.5 Emotional Law

The Official Anthem must evoke:

- belonging without comfort,
- intensity without chaos,
- purpose without certainty,
- resolve without closure.

It should not promise victory.  
It should promise **meaning**.

This anthem welcomes the Operative  
not as a hero,  
but as a participant.

---

### 4.6 Usage Mandate

The Official Resonantia Anthem may be used for:

- game startup and title screen,
- major trailers,
- milestone announcements,
- canon-defining transmissions,
- closing sequences and credits.

It must **not** be diluted through overuse.

When it plays,  
it must matter.

---

### 4.7 Canon Lock Rules ‚Äî Official Anthem

Once the Official Anthem is declared:

- Its **existence** is permanent
- Its **role** is immutable
- Its **identity** may not be replaced

Future versions may:
- expand arrangement,
- refine structure,
- improve production fidelity.

Future versions may **not**:
- contradict its emotional law,
- reduce its scope,
- fragment it into unrelated works.

---

### 4.8 Relationship to All Other Music

All other music in Project Dissonance is **downstream** from the Official Anthem.

- House Anthems inherit fragments of its language
- Card Tracks echo its timing philosophy
- System cues derive their gravity from it

If a piece of music conflicts with the Official Anthem,  
the anthem prevails.

## SECTION 5 ‚Äî PRODUCTION OBLIGATIONS

### 5.0 Purpose of Production Obligations

This section defines what is **mandatory** for any musical work  
declared within the Music Canon.

These obligations exist to ensure that:
- canon is respected,
- systems remain coherent,
- music can be integrated without reinterpretation,
- future Operatives inherit stable foundations.

This section does not dictate *style*.  
It dictates **completeness**.

---

### 5.1 Mandatory Deliverables (All Canon Tracks)

Every canon-declared track ‚Äî including:
- Card Tracks,
- House Anthems,
- the Official Resonantia Anthem,

must provide the following deliverables.

#### Required Assets

1. **Primary Master**
   - Lossless format (WAV or FLAC)
   - Full-length, uninterrupted version
   - No DJ intros or outros unless explicitly declared canon

2. **System-Ready Edit**
   - Trimmed or loop-safe version suitable for engine use
   - No abrupt starts or ends
   - Clean entry and exit points

3. **Track Metadata**
   - Canon track name
   - Associated Card / House / System
   - BPM (or BPM range)
   - Version identifier (v1.0, v1.1, etc.)

Without these, a track is considered **incomplete**.

---

### 5.2 Structural Integrity Requirements

All canon tracks must:

- maintain consistent tempo behavior within declared bounds,
- avoid unresolved clipping or distortion unless stylistically intentional,
- preserve clear rhythmic definition relevant to gameplay timing,
- respect declared energy roles (burst, sustain, disruption, etc.).

Experimental sound design is encouraged.  
Structural ambiguity is not.

---

### 5.3 Canon Compatibility

A track is considered **canon-compatible** only if:

- it fulfills its declared system role,
- it does not contradict the philosophy of its Card or House,
- it integrates cleanly with timing-driven systems.

A track may be musically impressive  
and still be **canon-invalid**.

Canon is not about taste.  
It is about alignment.

---

### 5.4 Versioning Rules

All canon tracks follow strict versioning.

- Initial declaration: **v1.0**
- Incremental refinements: **v1.1, v1.2, etc.**
- Major structural revision (same identity): **v2.0**

Version changes must:
- preserve core identity,
- respect Canon Lock Rules defined earlier,
- be logged in accompanying documentation.

Deleting or overwriting earlier versions is prohibited.

---

### 5.5 Prohibited Practices

The following are explicitly disallowed for canon tracks:

- placeholder tracks marked as final,
- reusing non-canon tracks without declaration,
- silent replacement of existing canon works,
- detaching music from its declared system role.

Violation of these rules breaks canon integrity  
and must be corrected before further production continues.

---

### 5.6 Responsibility and Accountability

Any Operative contributing music to Project Dissonance  
accepts responsibility for:

- understanding this Codex,
- delivering complete assets,
- respecting system requirements.

Failure to meet obligations does not disqualify the Operative.  
It disqualifies the **submission**.

Canon integrity always takes priority over speed.

---

### 5.7 Readiness for Workflow Binding

Once production obligations are understood and accepted,  
execution workflows may be applied.

The next section defines the **canonical production workflow**  
for the primary DAW environment.

This section defines the **canonical production workflow**  
for all music created under the Music Canon of Project Dissonance  
using **FL Studio Producer Edition 2024**.

The purpose is not to restrict creativity.  
It is to guarantee:

- reproducibility,
- consistency,
- system compatibility,
- future-proof collaboration.

Any track produced in FL Studio that claims canon status  
must adhere to this workflow.

---

For Prototype Phase I:

- **FL Studio Producer Edition 2024** is the sole canonical DAW.
- All reference workflows assume FL Studio behavior and structure.
- Other DAWs may be added later via parallel binding sections.

Tracks produced outside FL Studio during this phase  
may exist as sketches, but **cannot be declared canon**  
until they are conformed to this workflow.

### 6.4 FL Studio Session Requirements

Each FL Studio session must:

- use a **fixed BPM** within declared canon range,
- have tempo automation **explicitly documented** if used,
- avoid unresolved red clipping on the master channel,
- preserve clean bar alignment for drops and transitions.

Required Mixer Discipline:

- Master channel reserved for:
  - gentle limiting,
  - safety gain,
  - no creative distortion.
- Creative processing belongs on buses or channels, not Master.

---

### 6.5 Arrangement Discipline

Canon tracks must be arranged with **system awareness**.

This means:

- clear intro zone (even if aggressive),
- identifiable escalation points,
- readable rhythmic structure,
- intentional breakdowns or releases.

Random or unstructured arrangements  
may be musically interesting  
but are not canon-compatible.

---

### 6.6 Automation & Modulation Rules

Automation is encouraged  
but must obey the following:

- No automation may obscure the core pulse.
- Rhythmic clarity must be preserved.
- Automation clips must be named meaningfully.

Example:
FILTER_SWEEP_DROP_01
Unnamed automation is prohibited.

---

### 6.7 Export & Bounce Settings (Baseline)

While exact mastering chains may evolve,  
the following baseline rules apply:

- Sample Rate: **44.1 kHz or 48 kHz**
- Bit Depth: **24-bit**
- Dithering: **Off** unless final distribution requires it
- Normalize: **Off**
- Tail Rendering: **Enabled**

Silence trimming must be deliberate  
and not automatic.

---

### 6.8 Documentation Obligation

Each canon track must include  
a `Production_Notes.md` file containing:

- declared Card / House / System role,
- BPM and tempo behavior,
- version history summary,
- known limitations or future intents.

If the project cannot be understood  
without opening the FLP,  
it is insufficiently documented.

---

### 6.9 Canon Validation Checklist

Before a track may be declared canon-complete:

- [ ] File naming follows canon
- [ ] Folder structure is intact
- [ ] BPM matches declared range
- [ ] Required exports exist
- [ ] Notes are complete
- [ ] Track fulfills its system role

If any item fails,  
the track remains **non-canon**.

---

### 6.10 Future DAW Parity

Additional DAWs (LMMS, Ableton, Reaper, etc.)  
may be added in later phases.

Each will require:
- a dedicated workflow binding section,
- parity with FL Studio rules,
- explicit approval.

Until then,  
FL Studio remains the canonical execution environment.

## SECTION A ‚Äî CARD TRACK SOUND PALETTES

### A1 ‚Äî FEN-01_SURGE: KICK_SURGE LAW

#### Role & Intent

KICK_SURGE is not a groove element.  
It is the **pulse hammer** of Fenrir ‚Äî a forward-driving force that enforces momentum.

If a kick feels fun to dance to, it is incorrect.  
If it feels like it forces motion, it is correct.

KICK_SURGE represents inevitability, not enjoyment.

---

#### Shape Constraints (Non-Negotiable)

KICK_SURGE must exhibit:

- Fast transient
- Short to medium tail
- No extended decay
- No rounded or soft body

Explicit exclusions:

- No boomy techno kicks
- No long 909-style tails
- No round EDM-style kicks
- No click-only transient kicks

The kick must resolve fully before the next hit at 202 BPM.

---

#### Frequency Ownership

- Primary weight: ~50‚Äì65 Hz
- Punch zone: ~90‚Äì120 Hz
- Click presence: controlled, never sharp

Canonical rule:

> The kick owns low-end timing.  
> The bass owns low-end sustain.

In any conflict, the kick wins.

---

#### Mono & Spatial Law

- KICK_SURGE is 100% mono
- Center-positioned only
- No stereo widening
- No chorus
- No reverb on the main body

If widening feels necessary, the sound choice is invalid.

---

#### Emotional Validation

When soloed, KICK_SURGE must feel:

- Unmusical
- Unpleasant
- Inevitable

The correct response is not enjoyment, but submission to force.

### A2 ‚Äî FEN-01_SURGE: SUB_SURGE & BASS_SURGE RELATIONSHIP LAW

#### Role Separation (Absolute)

SURGE employs three low-frequency authorities:

- **KICK_SURGE** ‚Äî timing, command, impact  
- **SUB_SURGE** ‚Äî weight, pressure, mass  
- **BASS_SURGE** ‚Äî aggression, sustain, grind  

These roles must never blur.

If role boundaries collapse, power collapses.

---

#### Frequency Ownership (Non-Negotiable)

**SUB_SURGE**

- Operates below ~50 Hz
- No meaningful energy above ~70 Hz
- No midrange character
- Exists only to reinforce mass

**BASS_SURGE**

- Primary body between ~70‚Äì180 Hz
- Aggression harmonics above, controlled
- Must never compete with SUB_SURGE below ~50 Hz

Canonical hierarchy:

> SUB is weight.  
> BASS is aggression.  
> KICK is command.

---

#### Timing & Envelope Law

**SUB_SURGE**

- Always ducked or sidechained by KICK_SURGE
- Fast release, clean recovery
- Never overlaps kick transient

**BASS_SURGE**

- May overlap after kick impact
- Must never mask kick transient
- Allowed to swell, grind, or pulse only post-impact

Flab, wobble, or smear indicates violation of canon.

---

#### Mono & Stereo Law

- **SUB_SURGE**
  - 100% mono
  - Center only
  - No stereo processing under any circumstance

- **BASS_SURGE**
  - Low core remains mono
  - Stereo harmonics allowed above ~150 Hz only
  - Width is a controlled enhancement, never foundational

If low-end feels wide, the law is broken.

---

#### Emotional Validation

When isolated:

- SUB_SURGE must feel oppressive and heavy
- BASS_SURGE must feel angry and grinding

Combined with KICK_SURGE:

> The listener must feel forward pressure, not bounce.

### A3 ‚Äî FEN-01_SURGE: LEAD_SURGE & STAB_SURGE SYNTH LANGUAGE LAW

- Functions as a command signal

**STAB_SURGE**

- Delivers impact punctuation
- Reinforces drops and structural hits
- Confirms force rather than narrating it

LEAD points.  
STAB strikes.

They are not interchangeable.

---

#### Melodic Law (Strict)

**LEAD_SURGE**

- Minimal pitch movement
- Short motifs, never full melodies
- Repetition with intent
- Rhythm takes precedence over harmony

Allowed:

- 1‚Äì3 note phrases
- Stepwise motion
- Micro-variation

Forbidden:

- Long melodic runs
- Emotional chord progressions
- Heroic or singable lines

If it can be comfortably hummed, it violates canon.

---

**STAB_SURGE**

- No melodic movement
- Exists solely to reinforce rhythm and structure

STAB does not tell a story.

- Never anticipates the kick

**STAB_SURGE**

Playfulness in timing violates Fenrir law.

**STAB_SURGE**

Neither may mask:

- kick transient

Forbidden:

#### Emotional Validation

### A4 ‚Äî FEN-01_SURGE: FX_SURGE, NOISE_SURGE & CONTROL BOUNDARIES

#### Role of FX in SURGE

FX in SURGE exist to reinforce force, not decorate it.

They serve to:

- emphasize transitions,
- reinforce impact,
- clarify structure.

They must never:

- distract from rhythm,
- introduce narrative emotion,
- add groove or atmosphere.

If FX draw attention to themselves, they violate canon.

---

#### FX_SURGE Law (General FX)

FX_SURGE may include:

- short impacts,
- reverses,
- metallic hits,
- noise bursts.

Constraints:

- short duration,
- purpose-bound,
- event-triggered.

Forbidden:

- long risers,
- atmospheric beds,
- melodic FX,
- cinematic swells.

FX_SURGE reinforces moments, not space.

---

#### RISE_SURGE Law (Transition Control)

RISE_SURGE exists solely to:

- signal imminent structural change,
- build pressure before drops,
- prepare impact.

Rules:

- always terminates at a structural marker,
- never resolves softly,
- never bleeds into the drop.

Allowed:

- noise-based rises,
- filter sweeps,
- pitch ramps.

Forbidden:

- melodic risers,
- emotional crescendos,
- uplifting or cinematic behavior.

A rise must feel like tension tightening, not anticipation of joy.

---

#### IMPACT_SURGE Law (Structural Emphasis)

IMPACT_SURGE:

- coincides with DROP or PEAK,
- reinforces kick dominance,
- adds weight rather than brightness.

Rules:

- short and decisive,
- low‚Äìmid focused,
- never masks kick transient.

If the impact is heard more than felt, it is incorrect.

---

#### NOISE_SURGE Law (Controlled Chaos)

NOISE_SURGE exists to:

- add aggression texture,
- enhance brutality,
- introduce controlled chaos.

Rules:

- noise is shaped, not free,
- no constant noise beds,
- no wide stereo noise in the low range.

Allowed:

- gated noise,
- transient noise hits,
- rhythm-bound distortion textures.

Forbidden:

- ambience,
- wash,
- drift,
- uncontrolled stereo spread.

Noise must feel weaponized, not atmospheric.

---

#### Automation Boundary Law

Automation is permitted only if it:

- reinforces rhythm,
- clarifies structure,
- increases pressure.

Forbidden automation:

- slow emotional fades,
- ambient filter sweeps,
- cinematic build-ups.

Automation must feel mechanical and intentional.

---

#### Emotional Validation

When FX are soloed, they should feel:

- functional,
- harsh,
- unapologetic.

When FX are muted, the track must still function perfectly.

FX enhance SURGE; they never carry it.
